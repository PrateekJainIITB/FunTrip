Sindhi Koki Recipe

Are you looking to make something healthy for breakfast? Or perhaps you want to pack something nutritious in your children's tiffin? Try this Sindhi Koki recipe. It's simple to prepare and is bursting with flavors, making it a complete meal in itself. Let's start with this super tasty and amazing breakfast recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 1 spoon of whole coriander seeds
  • 1 spoon of whole cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cups of wheat flour
  • 1 or 2 big spoons of gram flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of caraway seeds
  • 1 spoon of crushed dry masala (coriander, cumin, black pepper)
  • Desi ghee, as needed
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pomegranate granule powder, as needed (or mango powder as a substitute)
  • Red chilli powder, to taste
  • Finely chopped green chillies, coriander leaves, and onion

Instructions:

  1. To make the dry masala, crush together whole coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and black pepper. You can also use powdered spices, but freshly ground masala offers a better taste.
  2. In a vessel, take two cups of wheat flour. Add one or two big spoons of gram flour, half a teaspoon of crushed caraway seeds, and one spoon of the prepared dry masala. If you prefer it spicier, add more masala as desired.
  3. Add desi ghee and salt to taste. Then, incorporate pomegranate granule powder. If unavailable, you can cook raw pomegranate in the oven, dry it, and grind into powder. Alternatively, use mango powder.
  4. Add finely chopped green chillies, a lot of coriander leaves, and finely chopped onion. Be cautious as adding onions and coriander can release water, making the mix soft.
  5. Knead the mixture into a dough using normal water. Avoid hot or cold water. The consistency should be similar to that of roti or chapatti dough. If using onions, reduce the water quantity and make the parathas immediately to prevent the dough from becoming too soft.
  6. Form small balls from the dough and flatten them slightly, like tikkis. Roast these on a dry pan or tawa for about 15 seconds on each side, ensuring they don't cook too much.
  7. Roll the semi-cooked dough balls with a rolling pin, sprinkling dry flour as needed. Roll them to a thickness slightly more than that of puris. Then, make cuts or designs on them with a knife.
  8. Heat a pan and cook the Koki. Apply ghee on both sides while roasting. You can use oil as an alternative to ghee. Since the Koki is thick, it will take time to cook.
  9. Serve the Sindhi Koki with yogurt or pickle, or enjoy it as is due to its rich flavor.

This tasty and healthy Sindhi Koki is perfect for breakfast or as a snack in tiffin. It's as delightful to look at as it is easy to make. Try this recipe and share it with your friends and family if you like it!
